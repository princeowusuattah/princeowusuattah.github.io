Overview

Halfex is a growing hostel, construction and property management that works in collaboration with universities to place students in their hostels and some private housing properties they manage. I was contacted by the team to develop dashboard concept for the management of their students and tenants applications as semester was about to end.



Background

Design brief

My main task was to research, conceptualize, ideate and visualize a high fidelity mockup on CMS platform that conveys the Halfex student-friendly values while making it human-centered and intuitive.


Going into this project my goal was to create a modular platform that users can tailor to their personal use. To be able to imagine all my ideas, I had to research deeply into Cloud-based SaaS, CMS applications and their principles.


How do I design a student hostel management platform that brings ease to the building, searching and browsing content?.

Solution

Solution

Halfex cloud is a web app for managing student housing details. Users can browse, search, filter applicant and tenant details. The user can customize the dashboard to their convenience. The card-centric visualization makes it easy for the users. The minimalistic and clean UI makes information digestible.



Simplified Content building

Users have access to a streamlined content builder that affords them easy creation of property and housing list


This helps to cut the time spent on adding a new list and editing draft list.


Search system

On all the individual dashboards, users can search globally and with the specific page using tag-based search. The search system also shows a stock of recently searched


searched items so users can minimize memory load. An effective search system enhances affordance and improves the overall user experience.


Onboarding Experience

he onboarding experience is simplified but continues using a tooltip to onboard users. Users at the early stage of the workflow are taken on a product


tour through key parts of the product using the tooltips. Parts of the product that are not consistent, but still introduces users in their next time of use.




Research

Research Methods

Secondary research

My initial actions were gathering thoughts and obtaining a comprehensive overview of goods and current pieces of literature in the subject of hostel administration. In order to establish some basic features on the market, I analyzed goods to grasp the user flows as well as the interface designs. I also went over papers that provided light on the gaps and hazards in existing products' user experiences.

 

One noteworthy result was that most of these managements used Excel as both a spreadsheet and a database. Conducting this preliminary study effectively set me on a road to better understanding the subject matter.

Stakeholder interviews

To better understand the issues and process of Halfex's user-employees, I performed a series of unstructured interviews virtually with two of them.

 

I also felt it necessary to interview two of my hostel managers in order to gain insight into their workflow and management style.
