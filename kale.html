What is Kale?

Kale is a social financial platform that lets people make verified products and service ratings whiles tracking their spendings across different payment systems. Verified ratings can be made when users purchases an item which is linked to their cards on the app. Similarly, they can track their spend on this same application.



Technology

How this project started

As a product driven designer, I like to explore different technologies and how to apply it to problems. In late 2017, this was the time I had come across Plaid, a api-driven solution that make it easy, safe and reliable for people to connect their financial data to apps and services.

Learning about the solution they provided led me to start thinking about how to use to address problem. Ratings and spending tracking seem like one of their best authetication feature so I started out to gain user requirements to create a visual design solution.

Background

The problems

Rating sites have a significant problem with "fake" reviews and ratings.Harvard Business School report reveals that 20 percent of reviews on Yelp are fake. This is a significant amount of reviews. Because rating sites haven't worked out how to validate every review, addressing this issue needs some rethinking.


Many millennials fail to preserve enough records of their prior financial behaviors in order to make more informed financial decisions in the future. Among millennials, social media is becoming the most effective tool for informing, forming habits, and guiding behavior. There is currently no tool that delivers financial analytics while smoothly integrating into millennials' social media habits.





Research

Research Methods

Assumptions and hypothesis

The user requirements were generated a list of hypothesis and assumptions. My objective, following the Lean UX philosophy, was to propose the requirements and later test them with users.

Analoguous products

I explored existing products that attempted to addressed problem.Because Yelp was obviously a first choice, flaws within the platform set up the stage for my ideations and sketching. Given that this project was a button up approach and limited in a few days I had to move face and get things done with little user input.



Design

Wireframing and User testing

I did a fast usability test with coworkers to identify design problems. They were tasked with completing three main activities using a prototype produced from wireframes. Participants were also asked to provide general input on the information layout and the features they would like to see on each screen.




Design

Solution

Getting started

After entering the app users can view people's post. Users can interact with post by commenting and liking post.



Giving reviews

Users can make verified reviews about products and services based on their payment system as it adds a layer of authentication. Reviews can be done with a five star rating scale and text to add context.



Spending tracking

Users can track their spending over multiple credit cards together all separately. The dashboard shows the highest spending and last spending, to draw users attention to their spending habit.



Deals

The platform curate deals for users who connects their payment option. The deals can be easily added by tapping the deal card.
