What is MediTrak

MediTrak Life is a telemedicine startup developing proprietory continuous tracking technology and digital therapeutics to help health care practitioners conduct precision medicine. Their flagship product, a vital sign tracker can collect multiple data that are commonly done is a hospital.



Background

Towards gaining better insights

Recognizing the significance of data insights from the first testing of the Vital signs monitoring device, the MediTrak chose to create an application that can house data, serve as an analytics platform, and provide greater insights to its customers. Because they were still in the early phases, they wanted to create a modular platform that would allow for simple future integration of additional features.


Solution

MediTrak Dashboard

The MediTrak Vital Sign platform is a cloud-based digital support system for MediTrak vital sign monitor device that offers physicians and care facilities ability to offer remotely health care by monitoring, storing, analysing and gaining insights from patients' vital signs over a period of time.

Over 27 million people with private insurance visit the emergency room, two-thirds of whom are "avoidable" and "not a real emergencies.".

The broader issue meditrak was attempting to solve was preventing unnecessary hospitalization by collecting actionable data so physicians can make time-sensitive decisions without any hospitalization."

Research

Research Methods

User Interviews

I kicked off the project by gaining a thorough understanding of the problem space in order to frame an actionable problem path. I started by sitting in on a meeting with the CEO, who also served as be the product manager, and two research scientists who were collaborators of the project.

Stakeholder meetings

I also interviewed two doctors and a senior living nurse, who served as project resources.Each interview lasted from one to two hours. I was interested in gaining a better view of the problem space and existing solutions.These interviews were more like an introduction than a straightforward investigation.



Thematic Analysis

Interview Insights

After the interviews were transcibed,I used the Job-to-be-Done Framework to thematically analysed the key insights in other to group the themes into user requirements. I narrowed it down to the most impactful feature, that would require minimum effort from the development team.

Design

Design Methods

Task Analysis

Having gathered all the information I needed, I went on to develop the initial flows. This was the stage most of the ideation was done on the specific designs.


This was done iteratively since I was getting feedback and critiques from all the team members. The major improvements I made were the name and titling of key features.


Ideation

The task analysis pointed me to various edge cases, which I attempted to investigate. For instance, the safety index was a notification system targeted at physicians to encourage them to prioritize.


The location of each tab was the focus of the most of the visual exploration for ideas. Iterations were carried out through guerrilla testing and feedback from the CEO and two additional research scientists.
