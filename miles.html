Challenge

Problem

I joined Miles just when it had launched its Refer-a-friend feature.Though people used it when it first launched, it started to decline over the weeks. Support tickets blew up on Zendesk. After after digging deep into customer feedback became apparent that the referral attribution had failed for some users and they weren’t receiving bonus miles after referrals.


35%

of the month's support tickets on Jira were referral attribution related.

22.5%

of the clients complained that they didn't receive their bonus miles after sharing with their friends, and their friend were also not getting their bonus when they signed up.



SOLUTION

How did we address it?

I led an end-to-end redesign of the Miles-refer-a-friend program's user experience, which involved the building of a multichannel referral system, multilevel authentication, and visual design and UX content overhaul with clear communication to reestablish trust among users and drive call-to-action.




Discovering the problem

Research

Analyzing support tickets and technical data

Miles takes support resquests through Zendesks and fowards it as tickets to Jira. After reviewing the Jira tickets, I found that 35% of all support tickets on Jira were referral attributions related. It was apparent that this was a technical issue. The backend wasn't attributing referees to their referrers. This meant that at least one of them wouldn't be rewarded. I started reviewing the current flows to understand the emerging issues further.


Current Miles RAF Workflow

Miles used branch.io to authenticate users and attribute new users entering the app. When a user onboards into the app for the first time, Branch validates the phone details to check if it is a new user or an old one; if new, we assign the user bonus miles.

When a user also comes through our refer-a-friend links, it does similar checks; Branch.io is not perfect, its efficacy rate is around 80%-90%, but since this feature was built lean to ship fast, it was robust enough and affordable at the time.



Problem with Attribution

Attribution can fail when there are bugs in the API calls. When it fails, it can start from the single webpage and when a user downloads the app there be no refferal bonue.

Branch can also fail to detect a device as new, which means they do not get a refferal bonus plus the orignal bonus miles gives to it's users.





Conducting in-app survey

We run a short survey to gain some quick insights about our users who had users the refer-a-friend within the past want. It asked questions regarding their experience, motivation, how they generally their felt about bonus miles and asked them to give any suggestion for improvement.

From their answers we understood that people were primarily interested in accruing more miles with little effort so referrals where a key way to achieve that.

"I thought this was a unique idea. Getting rewarded for me walking? I'm down for that."
"I like that I can even share miles with my friend through the referral. It is a great idea. I've discovered some interesting items from the rewards too."


Key takeaways - What motivation are users to share miles?

After the survey I reached out to about 20 power users for a quick informal interview, but only 5 made it. Through the interview, I sought to understand their: Motivation for sharing the app and uncover their mental model.This will help me map their user experience to suit their expectation

Uniqueness of the concept

Most users shared it because they thought it was a unique concept and wanted their family and friends to look at it. This was the most common theme in the analysis.

Accrue more miles

Users who identified themselves as been interested in rewards on the app shared with friends to accrue more miles.


Gamified experience

The gamified challenges around refer-a-friend encouraged some users to share with their friends.

Overall app experience

According to some of the users, they just shared because they generally liked the experience with the app.



Competitive Insights

A better way to find a product that could be apt for the target audience is through understanding how the competitors are doing currently in the market.I went on the analyze and understand what other competitors and benchmark products were building their RAF programs. Whiles this analysis was to find an innovative way of solving our problem, it was also to help the redesign to provide a competitive advantage over its peer products on the market. Some key opportunities appeared in the benchmarking.




Refferal codes

another channel of sharing. The ease of sending 5–10 limited characters was easy to share by the user and easy to validate at the back end.

User ID

User ID's also offered another layer of ease and authentication that users and our platform could leverage to solve this problem.

Refferal links

Referral links are generated along with the invitation for users to click and funnel into the app after down.




Defining opportunities

Synthesis

Defining the user journey

Throughout the project, I was reviewing and mapping out the current state journey. In other to extend how I empathized with the users with my team, I mapped it out using two personas' emotional journey. At every stage of the user journey, insights into the pain point were mapped and opportunities were suggested for further exploration. Benchmarked experiences learned from the competitive analysis help me make relevant tradeoffs. Specifically, I learned through all my analyses that promo codes were more popular and convenient with our customers because of their previous experiences with reward redemption.

Design Direction

Defining the problems to address

Given all pain points, It was necessary to narrow the scope to deliver solutions fast. Working in an agile environment, it was necessary to break it down into several phases to deliver the opportunities I found.



Perform auxiliary attribution checks

Building Auxilliary workflows that will authenticate attribution and other technology issues.

Provide multiple channels with ease

other channels need to be provided beyond the native share and the already provided channels.

Unify the User Experience

Providing a consistent experience (aesthetics & usability) end-to-end between the old user and the incoming users.

Build lost trust

Improve product messaging to build trust for the brand.
